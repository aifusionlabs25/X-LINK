Deploying an AI Sales Concierge for a home builder introduces high-stakes liability because real estate transactions are governed by strict federal and state consumer protection laws. When an AI "hallucinates," it isn't just a technical glitch; it is potentially a binding misrepresentation.

Liability Profile: The "Hallucination" Impact

If your AI provides incorrect data regarding interest rates, pricing, or timelines, the liability generally falls into three legal buckets:

1. UDAAP Violations (Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices)

Under the Dodd-Frank Act and FTC guidelines, providing false information about the cost of a "product" (the home) or the "financing" (the rate) is a primary trigger.

The Risk: Even if a human eventually corrects the AI, the "bait and switch" argument can be made if the buyer relied on that initial data to enter a contract or pay a reservation fee.

2. TILA/RESPA Non-Compliance

If the AI quotes a specific interest rate without the required disclosures (like the APR, down payment requirements, or repayment terms), it violates the Truth in Lending Act (TILA).

The Risk: Regulators do not distinguish between a typo made by a human and a hallucination made by an LLM. The builder could face statutory damages and "Right of Rescission" claims, where buyers may attempt to back out of contracts based on non-compliant disclosures.

3. Breach of Contract & Negligent Misrepresentation

In many jurisdictions, if an AI agent is authorized to "represent" the builder, its statements can be viewed as Equitable Estoppel.

The Risk: If a buyer signs a contract believing the "Base Price" was $450,000 (per the AI) but the contract says $500,000, the builder faces a PR nightmare and potential litigation for negligent misrepresentation.

3 Strict Guardrails for Prevention

To mitigate these risks, you must move beyond simple system prompts and implement structural technical barriers.

Guardrail 1: RAG with "Hard-Coded" Truth Anchors

Do not allow the AI to generate numbers from its internal weights. Use Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) connected to a verified SQL database or ERP (like Enertia or BuilderMT).

The Rule: If the API returns a null value for "Current Phase 2 Pricing," the AI must be programmed to say, "I don't have the live pricing for that lot yet," rather than guessing.

Verification: Implement a "NLI" (Natural Language Inference) check where a second, smaller model verifies that the AI's output numerically matches the source data before the message is sent to the user.

Guardrail 2: Dynamic Disclosure Injection

The AI should be incapable of mentioning a percentage sign (%) or a dollar sign ($) without an accompanying, legally-vetted disclaimer.

The Rule: Program a "Trigger-Response" layer. If the AI mentions a "4.9% rate," the system automatically appends: "Rate based on 740+ FICO, 20% down, and builder's preferred lender. See full disclosures at [URL]."

Guardrail 3: Human-in-the-Loop "Redline" Thresholds

Establish "High-Volatility" triggers that escalate the chat to a human agent immediately.

The Rule: Any query involving "Contract," "Interest Rate," "Closing Date," or "Discount" triggers a pre-formatted response or a mandatory transfer. The AI can act as a concierge for floor plans and amenities, but must be "lobotomized" regarding specific financial commitments.
